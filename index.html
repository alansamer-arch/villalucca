02 — Now Showing

Brunch at
the Villa.

At Villa Lucca, brunch isn't a meal — it's a standing appointment. This film sets the table in motion around its centerpiece: honey poured warm and slow over a golden, flaky crust, the cheese board built for sharing, and the easy, unhurried company that gathers around it.

It was made to carry a feeling, not just a plate — the slow, all-day table the house is known for. The pour, the board and the laughter around it do the inviting — come, sit, share, stay.

03 — Also Showing

Cream Brûlée
French Toast.

Some dishes are made to be ordered; others are made to be understood. This film brings the Cream Brûlée French Toast into the light — quick, tactile cuts of the make: the torch, the crack of the caramel crust, the slow pull of the inside — and then the first bite.

It was built to answer something real: guests didn't always know how to approach the dish, or how to finish it. So the film teaches the want — the inside, the texture, the way it's meant to be eaten — in the most entertaining, editorial way.
